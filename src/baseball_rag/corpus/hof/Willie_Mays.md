Willie Mays, nicknamed "The Say Hey Kid," was one of the most electrifying players in baseball history during his 22-season career with the New York Giants and San Francisco Giants. He made his major league debut in 1951 at age 20 and immediately captivated fans with his spectacular defensive play and powerful hitting. Mays won the Rookie of the Year award that season and helped lead the Giants to the National League pennant, beginning a career that would see him accumulate 3,283 hits, 660 home runs, and 338 stolen bases.

Mays was renowned for his signature "basket catch," a style where he would catch fly balls with his glove positioned above his head rather than in front—a technique he perfected through extraordinary instinct and hand-eye coordination. His defensive prowess in center field was matched by few in baseball history; he could track down balls in the gaps with speed that seemed to defy physics and throw out runners from any angle with his powerful arm. He won 12 consecutive Gold Glove Awards from 1957 to 1968, a testament to his consistency in the field.

The 1954 World Series showcased Mays at his absolute best. In Game 1 against the Cleveland Indians, he made perhaps the greatest defensive play in World Series history—the famous "catch" where he sprinted deep into center field and snagged Vic Wertz's drive before crashing into the wall. The Giants went on to sweep the series in a major upset. Mays was named MVP that season, one of two times he would earn the award during his career.

Mays retired in 1973 after 22 seasons with a .302 career batting average, 660 home runs, and 1,909 RBIs. He was elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1979 on the first ballot and remains beloved as one of the most complete players in baseball history—a five-tool star who could hit for power and average while fielding at an elite level.
